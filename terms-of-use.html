You should always consult your doctor before starting any new exercise program. This is particularly true if any of the following apply to you: 

You experience chest pain
You feel faint of have spells of dizziness
You have joint of bone problems 
You have high blood pressure
You have a heart condition
You are pregnant
You are currently taking medication 
You are not used to being very physically active

If none of these apply to you can be reasonably sure that you can become more physically active. If you begin to experience any of the physical symptoms listed above or your health situation changes you should contact your doctor immediately. 

If any of the above conditions apply to you then you should consult your doctor before becoming more physically active. Tell your doctor which of the conditions applied to you and explain a little about the kind of activity you want to participate in. You may be able to do any activity you want-as long as you begin slowly and build up gradually, or you may need to restrict your activities to those which are safe for you.
